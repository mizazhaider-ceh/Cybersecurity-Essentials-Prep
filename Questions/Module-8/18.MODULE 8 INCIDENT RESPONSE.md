**Question 65** (ğŸ”´ **CRITICAL - PREPARATION!**)

**IRP - Preparation Phase:**

**Prepares organization PRIOR to incident:**

Questions answered:

- **How will we handle incidents?**
- **Who do we communicate to/escalate to?**
- **Criteria for reporting to police/authorities?**
- **Who is on Incident Response Team? How/when activate?**
- **Where do we gather to handle incident?**
- **Which equipment needed?** (Prepare in advance!)

**Key principle:** All planning done **BEFORE** incident occurs!

***

**Question 66** (ğŸ”´ **CRITICAL - IDENTIFICATION!**)

**IRP - Identification Phase:**

**Designed to verify incident occurred and gather details:**

Contains:

- Verifying reports/events qualify as incident
- Assignment of incident **"type"** (ENISA/US-CERT classification)
- Assigning **ownership** of incident to incident handler
- Determining **severity** and escalating as necessary
- Establishing **"chain of custody"** when handling potential evidence

**What is "chain of custody"?**

**Answer:** Documentation of how evidence is handled (defined in forensics section)

***

**Question 67** (ğŸ”´ **CRITICAL - CONTAINMENT!**)

**IRP - Containment Phase:**

**Aims to limit exposure (and effects) of incident:**

Contains:

- **Activating Incident Response Team**
- **Notifying stakeholders** (business owners of affected systems)
- **Obtaining agreement** on actions affecting availability/risk
- **Getting IT representatives** involved to implement containment
- **Implementing containment measures:**
    - Disconnecting from Internet
    - Hibernation
    - Etc.
- **Obtaining and preserving evidence**
- **Documenting actions** from this phase onward
- **Controlling public communication** (PR team when needed)

**Why obtain agreement from business owners?**

**Answer:** Actions may affect availability of service or create risk

***

**Question 68** (ğŸ”´ **CRITICAL - ERADICATION!**)

**IRP - Eradication Phase:**

**Designed to determine and eradicate root cause:**

Contains:

- **Determining signs and root cause** of incident
- **Locating most recent backups** and installing OR considering alternatives (like reinstalling OS)
- **Removing root cause** of incident
    - Example: Worm/virus â†’ deploy patches and updated antivirus
- **Improving defenses** by implementing/fine-tuning security controls
- **Performing vulnerability analysis** to find new vulnerabilities introduced by root cause

**Match:** "Deploying patches to remove worm infection" = ?

**Answer:** Eradication phase activity

***

**Question 69** (ğŸ”´ **CRITICAL - RECOVERY!**)

**IRP - Recovery Phase:**

**Ensures affected systems/services restored to specified state:**

**Time from incident to end of this phase = RTO!**

Includes:

- **Restoring operations** to specified state
- **Validating** actions taken on restored systems successful
- **Getting involvement** of system owners to test their systems
- **System owners declare normal operation**

**What metric defines recovery phase timeframe?**

**Answer:** RTO (Recovery Time Objective)
