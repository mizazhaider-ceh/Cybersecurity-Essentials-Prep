**Question 65** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - PREPARATION!**)

**Designed to verify incident occurred and gather details:**

Contains:

**Question 67** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - CONTAINMENT!**)

Contains:

- **Activating Incident Response Team**
- **Notifying stakeholders** (business owners of affected systems)
- **Obtaining agreement** on actions affecting availability/risk
- **Getting IT representatives** involved to implement containment
- **Implementing containment measures:**
    - Disconnecting from Internet
    - Hibernation
    - Etc.
- **Obtaining and preserving evidence**
- **Documenting actions** from this phase onward
- **Controlling public communication** (PR team when needed)

**Answer:** Actions may affect availability of service or create risk

***

- **Removing root cause** of incident
    - Example: Worm/virus ‚Üí deploy patches and updated antivirus
- **Improving defenses** by implementing/fine-tuning security controls
- **Performing vulnerability analysis** to find new vulnerabilities introduced by root cause

**Question 101** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - INVESTIGATION DEFINITION!**)

**Definition:**

- Collection and analysis of evidence with goal of **identifying perpetrator** of incident

- **Investigations:** May require attack to **continue** while analyzed and evidence collected

**Reason for investigation:**

- Contract violation
- Policy violation

**Who investigates?**

- In-house teams
- Specialized companies
- Law enforcement
- Regulators

**Question 126** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - DEFINITION \& PHASES!**)

**Digital Forensics:**

**Definition:**

- Process of **identifying, preserving, analyzing, and presenting** digital evidence in manner **legally acceptable** in legal proceeding

**Four Phases:**

1. **Identify**
2. **Preserve**
3. **Analyze**
4. **Present**

**Process flow:**

```
Media ‚Üí Data ‚Üí Information ‚Üí Evidence
```

**How many phases in digital forensics?**

**Answer:** 4 phases

***

**Question 127** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - FORENSIC PHASES DETAIL!**)

**Forensic Investigation Phases:**

**1. Identify:**

- Identifying **location** of available information that may constitute evidence

**2. Preserve:**

- Practice of **retrieving** identified information and **preserving** as evidence
- Example: **"Imaging"** (bit-by-bit copy) of original media with witness present
- **Chain of custody must be documented**

**3. Analyze:**

- **Extracting, processing, interpreting** evidence
- Make understandable for **layperson** (e.g., lawyer)

**4. Present:**

- Present to management, attorneys, court, etc.

**Match:** "Creating bit-by-bit copy of hard drive with witness" = ?

**Answer:** Preserve phase

***

**Question 128** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - DATA PROTECTION!**)

**Key Elements of Digital Forensics:**

**DATA PROTECTION:**

- IRP needs description of how to inform parties to **protect potential evidence**
- Example: **Do NOT disconnect power or wires**

**DATA ACQUISITION:**

- Get all data on media (hard disk, removable media) in **controlled location**
- Gather **data stored in RAM** if possible
    - **Disappears when computer shut down**
    - Examples: who was logged on
    - **Store safely (hibernation!)**
- **Write-block media** (specialized hardware encasing hard disk)
- **Recording of witnesses** (voice recorder)

**Why is RAM data collection critical?**

**Answer:** RAM data disappears when system powers down

***

**Question 129** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - IMAGING!**)

**IMAGING:**

**Definition:**

- **Bit-for-bit copy** of data (e.g., hard disk) to keep original **unchanged**
- Do analysis on **copy**, not original

**Advantages:**

- Allows analysis of **"hidden" zones** of hard disk
- Zones where no files visible in Windows Explorer
- In extreme cases, data recovered from disk **even when erased or overwritten** (using specialized tools!)

**Why not analyze original disk?**

**Answer:** Keep original unchanged for evidence integrity; work on copy

***

**Question 130** (üü° Medium - **EXTRACTION \& NETWORK ANALYSIS!**)

- Identifying and selecting **relevant data** on copy (image) of device

**NETWORK TRAFFIC ANALYSIS:**

- Identify patterns in network communications:
    - **Where** traffic taking place
    - **When** and **for how long** communications occur
    - **Size** of information transferred

**INTERVIEWS:**

- Gather information to recognize:
    - Telephone numbers
    - Names of individuals
    - Etc. in extracted data

**Purpose of interviews in forensics?**

**Answer:** Help recognize/interpret data found in evidence

**Question 131** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - LOG FILE ANALYSIS!**)

**LOG FILE ANALYSIS:**

**Challenge:**

- Log files contain **vast amounts** of evidence data
- **Difficult to analyze** manually

**Solution: Automatic tools (possibly through SIEM):**

**1. Audit Reduction Tools:**

- Removes records known to be **irrelevant** for security investigation
- Example: Nightly backup records

**2. Trend and Variance Detecting Tools:**

- Looks for **anomalies** in user/system behavior
- Example: User logs on daily at 9h00, but one day at 4h00

**3. Attack-Signature-Detection Tools:**

- Looks for **sequences of events** constituting attack
- Example: Repeated failed login attempts on various PCs in same office

**Match:** "Tool removing nightly backup entries from logs" = ?

**Answer:** Audit Reduction Tool

***

**Question 132** (üü° Medium - **FORENSIC TOOL CATEGORIES!**)

**1. Computer Forensic Tools:**

- Examine **permanent memory** (hard drives, USB sticks, CDs)

**2. Memory Forensic Tools:**

- Acquiring and exploring **volatile memory (RAM)**

**3. Mobile Devices:**

- Mostly **hardware** (write-blockers \& duplicators)

**4. Network Forensic Tools:**

- Monitoring and analysis of **network traffic**

**TIMELINES:**

- Order events on timeline:

1. Look for **relations between events**

**Question 133** (üü° Medium - **FINAL FORENSIC ELEMENTS!**)

**REPORTING:**

**Answer:** Make findings understandable to non-technical investigators

***

**Question 134** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - ANTI-FORENSICS!**)

**ANTI-FORENSICS:**

**Definition:**

- Ways used by cybercriminals to make data retrieval **more difficult or impossible** for investigators

**Methods:**

**1. Data Deletion by Overwriting:**

- Makes recovery extremely difficult

**2. Encryption:**

**3. Hiding Data in Unused Space:**

**4. Steganography:**

**Answer:** Hiding data in plain sight (e.g., invisible ink, data in image files)

**Question 135** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - STEGANOGRAPHY EXAMPLE!**)

**Steganography Examples:**

**Classic:**

- Writing letter in **invisible ink** on paper with visible text

**Computer:**

- Hide file/message in **another file**

**Simple text example:**

**Image steganography:**

- Store hidden data
- **Invisible to human eye!**

**Example in slides:**

- Using LSB of RGB values to encode ASCII characters

**True or False:** "Steganography can hide messages in image files invisibly."

**Answer:** True

**Question 161** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - DISASTERS DEFINITION!**)

**Disasters:**

**Definition:**

- Disruptions causing **critical information resources** to be **inoperative** for period of time

**"Period of time":**

- Could be **minutes to months**

**Require:**

- **"Recovery" effort** to get back to normal business
- **Way to "continue business"** until fully recovered

**Types:**

**Natural:**

- Earthquake, flood, tornado, fire
- **Covid falls in this category!**

**Man-made:**

- Terrorist attack, strike
- Large hacker attack (ransomware)
- Significant virus infection
- Human error (accidental database deletion)

**Is ransomware a natural or man-made disaster?**

**Answer:** Man-made

***

**Question 162** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - BCP vs DR!**)

**Business Continuity vs Disaster Recovery:**

**Business Continuity:**

- Focuses on **keeping business running** during and immediately after incident
- **WITHOUT IT if needed**

**Disaster Recovery:**

- Focuses on **post-incident period**
- **How to return to pre-incident status** in terms of IT infrastructure and data

**Example:**

- **Fire damages office building:**
    - **Business Continuity:** Ask employees to work from home
    - **Disaster Recovery:** Provide same IT infrastructure/data (possibly different location)
    - **Also use:** Incident Response Plan!

**Which focuses on keeping business running WITHOUT IT?**

**Answer:** Business Continuity

***

**Question 163** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - BCP ELEMENTS!**)

**Business Continuity Plan (BCP):**

**Takes into account:**

1. **What are critical operations** needed to survive?
2. **Which material and human resources** necessary for critical operations?
3. **What are criteria** for declaring disaster?
    - Not every incident is disaster
    - **Every disaster IS an incident**
4. **Who is responsible** for what exactly?
5. **Which contracts** in place?
    - Emergency office
    - Computer rental
    - Moving company
    - Etc.

**First two questions answered by:**

- **Business Impact Analysis (BIA)**

**True or False:** "Every disaster is an incident, but not every incident is a disaster."

**Answer:** True

***

**Question 164** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - BIA STEPS!**)

**Business Impact Analysis (BIA):**

**Basis for developing BCP.**

**Steps:**

**1.** Find **all business processes** in company (e.g., sales, marketing)

**2.** Which processes are **critical for survival**?

**3.** Which **information resources** linked to critical processes? (servers, people)

**4.** Which **threats** exist for these processes?

- With which **probability** and **impact**?
- How good are our **(security) controls**?
- **Note:** This refers to **Risk Analysis!**

**5.** How much **data can we afford to lose** for each process? (**RPO**)

**6.** What is **maximum recovery time** before unacceptable losses? (**RTO**)

**Which step connects BIA to Risk Analysis?**

**Answer:** Step 4 (threats, probability, impact, controls)

***

**Backup and Recovery:**

**Critical principle:**

- **No backup ‚Üí No RPO possible!**

**Backup procedure considers:**

**1. Backup medium/media:**

- Disk, tape, cloud, or mix

**2. Location:**

- **Offsite and onsite** backup

**3. Frequency:**

**Question 170** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - BACKUP RULES!**)

**Backup Best Practices:**

**3-2-1 Rule:**

- Keep at least **3 copies** of your data
- Keep **2 copies** on different storage media
- Keep **1 copy offsite** (cloud or physical location)

**3-2-2 Rule:**

- Same as 3-2-1 except **two copies offsite**

**3-2-1-1-0 Rule:**

- Plus at least **1 copy offline** (air-gapped)
- **0 errors** (all backups regularly tested for recoverability!)

**Ransomware-proof requirement:**

**What does "immutable" backup mean?**

**Answer:** Backup that cannot be modified or deleted (ransomware-proof)

### **‚ö° CRITICAL CONCEPTS:**

**Event vs Incident:**

**Incident Classification:**

**Incident Response Phases (6):**

1. **Preparation** - Plan before incident
2. **Detection \& Analysis** - Identify incident
3. **Investigation** - Identify perpetrator (if needed)
4. **Containment** - Limit damage
5. **Eradication \& Recovery** - Remove threat, restore systems
6. **Post-incident Activity** - Lessons learned

**Evidence Handling:**

- **Chain of Custody** = Document all handling
- **Copy hard disk \& RAM FIRST** (before reboot!)
- Legal requirements vary by country
- International incidents = potential conflicts

**Digital Forensics (4 phases):**

1. **Identify** - Locate evidence
2. **Preserve** - Retrieve and protect (imaging!)
3. **Analyze** - Extract and interpret
4. **Present** - Report findings

**Anti-Forensics:**

- Data deletion (overwriting)
- Encryption
- Hiding data (slack space)
- **Steganography** (hiding in plain sight)

**BCP vs DR:**

- **BCP** = Keep business running (even without IT)
- **DR** = Restore IT infrastructure/data
- **BIA** = Identify critical operations, resources, threats

**Backup Methods:**
