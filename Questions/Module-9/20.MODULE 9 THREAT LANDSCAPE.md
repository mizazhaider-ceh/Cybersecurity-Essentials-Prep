**Question 1** (ðŸ”´ **CRITICAL - DEFINITION!**)

**Threat Landscape:**

**Definition:**

- **Comprehensive snapshot** of current cybersecurity threat environment
- **Aggregates and synthesizes** threat intelligence data from dozens of sources (feeds, incident reports, surveys, etc.)
- **Presents macro-level trends**, emerging risks, and threat actor behaviors across industries and geographies

- **Identify trends:** Dominant attack vectors, top malware families, threat actors

- **Inform strategic decisions** in security architecture, compliance, and defense prioritization

## **PART D: PYRAMID OF PAIN \& MITRE ATT\&CK (Questions 91-120)**


***

**Question 91** (ðŸ”´ **CRITICAL - PYRAMID DEFINITION!**)

**Pyramid of Pain:**

**Developed:** 2013

**Purpose:**

- Helps cybersecurity professionals **understand and categorize "Indicators of Compromise" (IoCs)**
- Based on **how much they disrupt attackers' operations**

**Indicator of Compromise (IoC):**

- Anything that can be **detected** and that is or can be a **sign** that your system is **compromised** by an attack

**Organization:**

- Six levels from **least to most impactful** on attacker

**Key Insight:**

- **Detecting attacks based on hash values** = Much easier for attacker to circumvent
- **Detecting attacks based on TTPs** = MUCH HARDER for attacker to change

**What year was Pyramid of Pain developed?**

**Answer:** 2013

***

**Question 92** (ðŸ”´ **CRITICAL - PYRAMID LEVELS!**)

**Pyramid of Pain - Six Levels (Bottom to Top):**


| Level | Detection Difficulty | Impact on Attacker |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **1. Hash Values** | Trivial | Easy (for attacker to change) |
| **2. IP Addresses** | Simple | Simple (for attacker to change) |
| **3. Domain Names** | Easy | Easy (for attacker to change) |
| **4. Network/Host Artifacts** | Annoying | Annoying (for attacker to change) |
| **5. Tools** | Challenging | Challenging (for attacker to change) |
| **6. TTPs** | Tough! | **PAINFUL** for attacker to change! |

**Explanation:**

**Bottom (Easy to detect, easy for attacker to evade):**

- **Hash values:** Change one byte â†’ new hash
- **IP addresses:** Get new IP easily
- **Domain names:** Register new domain

**Top (Hard to detect, PAINFUL for attacker to change):**

- **TTPs (Tactics, Techniques, Procedures):** Attacker's entire methodology
- Changing TTPs requires **complete retooling and retraining**!

**What is at the TOP of the Pyramid of Pain?**

**Answer:** TTPs (Tactics, Techniques \& Procedures)

***

**Question 93** (ðŸ”´ **CRITICAL - TTPs DEFINITION!**)

**TTPs = Tactics, Techniques \& Procedures**

**Why are TTPs at top of pyramid?**

- **MOST PAINFUL** for attacker to change
- Represents attacker's **entire approach** and **methodology**
- Changing TTPs requires:
    - Complete **retooling**
    - **Retraining** of personnel (if organized group)
    - **New development** of attack methods
    - Significant **time and resources**

**Example:**

- **Hash value:** Change one byte of malware â†’ new hash (trivial)
- **TTP:** Change from phishing-based attacks to exploiting zero-days â†’ requires completely different skillset, tools, and approach (PAINFUL!)

**Defending based on TTPs = Most effective but also most challenging!**

**What does TTP stand for?**

**Answer:** Tactics, Techniques \& Procedures

- **Learning:** **NO** learning from input
- **Example:** Medical diagnosis system where doctor programmed all rules
- **Demo:** symptomate.com

- **Decision rules:** Created by **computer through learning**
- **Features:** **Extracted by AI itself** (we DON'T tell it what to look for!)
- **Learning:** **YES** - learns from training data
- **Neural Networks:** More complex than ML
- **Example:** Image recognition that discovers "car has 4 wheels" by itself
- **Demo:** TensorFlow

**Example:**

- **Machine Learning:** We tell AI "car has 4 wheels, bike has 2"
- **Deep Learning:** AI **discovers** on its own that cars have 4 wheels!

**What's the key difference between ML and Deep Learning?**

**Answer:** Deep Learning extracts features itself; ML needs humans to provide features
