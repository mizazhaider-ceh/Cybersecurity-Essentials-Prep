**Question 171** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - ACTUAL EXAM MATH!**)

**Password entropy formula:**

$$
E = L \times \log_2(R) \text{ bits}
$$

Where:

- **E** = entropy in bits
- **L** = length (number of characters)
- **R** = character set size

**Example:** Password "IncoRRect77\$%\&"
**L** = 14
**R** = 94 (uppercase, lowercase, numbers, special symbols)

**Calculate entropy:**

$$
E = 14 \times \log_2(94) \approx 14 \times 6.5545 \approx 91.76 \text{ bits}
$$

**Question:** Is 91.76 bits entropy sufficient for **important users** (require 60-80 bits)?

**Answer:** **Yes** (91.76 > 80 bits)

***

**Question 172** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - ACTUAL EXAM CALCULATION!**)

**Calculate:**

$$
11 \times \log_2(52) = 62.7048369 > 60 \text{ bits}
$$

**Question:** Using **Hebrew alphabet** (22 characters), how long must password be for **60 bits minimum**?

**Answer:** **14 characters minimum**

**Calculation:**

$$
14 \times \log_2(22) = 62.43204266 > 60 \text{ bits}
$$ 

***

**Question 173** (üî¥ **CRITICAL - ACTUAL EXAM CALCULATION!**)

**Password length = exactly 16 characters**. For **crucial user** (100 bits minimum), what minimum **character set size** needed?

**Calculation:**

$$
16 \times \log_2(R) = 100
$$

$$
\log_2(R) = \frac{100}{16} = 6.25
$$

$$
R = 2^{6.25} = 76.11
$$

**Round UP** to exceed 100 bits ‚Üí **minimum 77 different characters**

**Answer:** **77 characters**
